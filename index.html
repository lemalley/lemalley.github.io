A Brief Background

I am a one who has long suffered from the desperate need for perfection. Straight lines, clean layout, evenly spaced text- these have always been goals to achieve in school posters and projects since elementary school. I always wanted everything to look as professional as possible. However, it wasn't until I had finished my college years and my achademic goals had been completed that I was developed the confidence to pursue my curiosity of learning Adobe's Illustrator or InDesign programs. With the help of my younger brother (no shame!), I got my feet wet. It began with a specific project (as most of my challenges have arisen).

I was putting together a reference book for a dear friend of mine's birthday, but it needed a professional cover!

Side Note: If you are unfamiliar with Dorothy Dunnett's work, then for all that is good in this world, please do yourself a massive favor and remedy that right away by clicking here. Do it. Do it now.

From this first project, there came many, many more. If I didn't know how to do something, I googled it. Google is a designer' (and coder's, I'm now learning) best friend. Any reason I found for doing any kind of design project, I dove into ID and/or AI further.
